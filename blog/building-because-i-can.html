Building Tools Because I Can, Not Because I Have To

November 2025 Philosophy Michael Lee Fluharty

After retiring from computer electronics repair, I found a new passion in app development. Not out of necessity, but out of curiosity and the joy of creation. Every app I build solves a problem I've personally encountered or addresses a need I've identified.

This approach keeps development fun and meaningful. I'm not chasing trends or building for the masses‚ÄîI'm creating tools that I actually use, and if others find them useful too, that's a bonus.

The Freedom of Retirement Development

Retirement gave me something invaluable: time without pressure. I'm not racing to meet deadlines, impress investors, or chase unicorn valuations. I build what interests me, when it interests me.

This freedom has made me a better developer. Without the pressure to ship fast and iterate faster, I can focus on quality, thoughtful design, and actually solving problems properly.

Solving My Own Problems

The best software comes from scratching your own itch. Every app in my portfolio started with a simple thought: "I wish I had a tool that could do this."

  • TakeAChanceOnMe - I needed a simple random number generator across all my devices
  • Tally Matrix Clock - I wanted a unique visual clock for my Apple TV
  • wWw NGPortal - I wanted to self-host without the complexity of traditional server setups

Each project is personal. Each one solves a real problem I've experienced.

The Joy of Creation

There's something deeply satisfying about building things. Taking an idea from concept to reality, seeing it work, and then actually using it in your daily life‚Äîthat's the reward.

Money and downloads are nice, but they're not why I do this. I build because I can, because it's fun, and because creating things brings me joy.

Advice for Others

If you're thinking about starting your own development journey, my advice is simple: build what you need. Don't worry about market size or monetization strategies. Just solve a problem you have, make it work well, and share it.

The rest will follow naturally.
